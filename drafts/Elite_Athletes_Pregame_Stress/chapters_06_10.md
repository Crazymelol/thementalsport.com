## Chapter 9: The Stoic Approach

### Controlling the Controllables

**Internals (You Control):**
- Your preparation
- Your effort
- Your attitude
- Your breathing
- Your self-talk

**Externals (You Don't Control):**
- Weather
- Referees
- Crowd
- Opponents
- Delays

**The Rule:** Attack the internals. Ignore the externals.

### CASE STUDY: Simone Biles - The Mental Health Pioneer

**Sport:** Gymnastics  
**Olympic Medals:** 7

**Tokyo 2020 Experience:**
Biles withdrew from multiple events due to "the twisties"—a mental block causing loss of spatial awareness mid-air.

**Her Approach:**
- Daily therapy sessions
- Prioritized mental health over medals
- Spoke openly about anxiety and pressure
- Started competition days with therapy

**The Impact:**
Biles' decision normalized mental health in elite sports and showed that champions prioritize well-being over performance.

**Key Quote:**
"I have to focus on my mental health and not jeopardize my health and well-being. We have to protect our minds and our bodies."

**Lesson:**
Mental health support isn't weakness—it's performance enhancement.
