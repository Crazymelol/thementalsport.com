## Diagnosis
- **Flying blind on metrics.** `STATUS.md`: _"could not find profile link on
  Pinterest home (header not loaded)."_ No impressions, saves, outbound-click, or
  pin-view numbers. Do **not** infer any pin is "working" or "failing."
- **What's posted:** 7 pins (short-001 → short-007), all repurposed from the Shorts
  bank. The titles are the *video* titles — curiosity hooks written for a swipe
  feed ("LeBron Doesn't Calm Down", "Stop Playing For Stats"). Right for YouTube,
  wrong for Pinterest.
- **Keyword/design gap (one sentence):** the pins are reposted video captions —
  curiosity titles with literal `#Shorts` and hashtag-stuffed descriptions —
  instead of search assets built around the phrases a worried parent or anxious
  athlete actually types into the Pinterest search bar.
- **Bottleneck = discoverability + click-through.** "LeBron Doesn't Calm Down"
  contains zero terms anyone searches on Pinterest. With no search-matching
  keywords in the title or first 1–2 description lines, pins never surface, never
  earn the save, never compound — the entire point of the platform. Pinterest also
  *rewards* the outbound link (unlike X), so leaving search demand on the table is
  the costly miss.

**Universal description fixes (all pins):**
- **Drop `#Shorts` from the title** — a YouTube artifact wasting prime keyword space.
- **Lead the description with the search phrase, not the emoji.** The first ~50
  characters are what Pinterest weighs and shows in-feed. Keywords first, then the
  soft CTA.
- **Hashtags are near-dead weight on Pinterest** — keywords in title/description/
  board name are the search mechanism. Spend the characters on natural language.

## Pin design / board actions

  third ("WHAT TO SAY AFTER A GAME", "PRE-GAME NERVES").

  parenting boards; athlete pins (002, 004, 005, 007) → mindset/nerves boards.

  items, drip ~1–2 *fresh* pins/day rather than re-pinning the same 7.
- **Multiple pins per topic** — one script can spawn 2–3 pins (different images/
  titles) targeting different queries; multiplies search surface for free.
