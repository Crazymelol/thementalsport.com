## Diagnosis (the honest version)

The numbers: recent Shorts get **6–8 views** (LeBron=8, You-Finally-Won=6,
You're-So-Talented=6). Your older winners — a **Mike-Tyson-thumbnail Short
(1,180)** and "Nurturing Self-Worth" **(385)** — crushed them. Average view
duration is **0:08** on ~50-second videos.

Three things are true, in order of leverage:

1. **The first 2 seconds kill you.** On Shorts there is no thumbnail — the *first
   frame + first sentence ARE the packaging*. Yours opens on a static wall of
   uppercase text with a gentle fade. By the time it's readable, the viewer has
   swiped. `0:08` is that swipe. This is the #1 lever and it's a *format* problem,
   not a writing problem.
2. **The algorithm has stopped trusting the channel.** 6–8 views means YouTube is
   barely testing your Shorts to anyone — because channel-wide `0:08` retention
   taught it not to. You re-earn distribution with a few genuinely high-retention
   videos, not with volume of low-retention ones.
3. **Recognizable specificity wins — and your own data proves it.** Your best
   recent Short (LeBron, 8) and your all-time winner (Tyson, 1,180) both led with
   a *name people know*. Abstract ones ("You're So Talented", parents) lagged.

What is NOT broken: your hooks are actually decent ("You finally won… so why do
you feel empty?", "The crowd is gone. You just lost. Now what?"). The writing
isn't the problem — the *visual delivery* of it is. And the backend (QA gate,
cloned voice) is fine. Stop polishing those; they're not the bottleneck.

## The fix, in order

### 1. First-frame retention (the prototype — needs your greenlight)
Rework the hook screen so frame 1 already shows the *payoff or a big number*, with
motion — not a slow-building text wall. A prototype is on the `claude/sleepy-gauss`
branch (rendered, not posted). See the side-by-side I'll send you.

### 2. Lead with a name in frame 1
Where a hook references someone known (LeBron, Giannis Antetokounmpo, Tyson),
put that **name/number as the very first thing on screen and the first word
spoken**. It's your proven stop-pattern.

### 3. Hook tightening — front-load the punch
Your hooks are good but bury the hit. Cut the runway so the surprising part lands
in word one or two:

| Item | Current first line | Tighter open | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| short-005 | "If you're anxious before competition — good. Let me explain." | "Pre-game panic is a *good* sign. Here's the science." | kills "let me explain" runway; leads with the contrarian claim |
| short-007 | "Giannis doesn't play for stats. Here's what he plays for instead." | "Giannis Antetokounmpo ignores his own stats. On purpose." | name first; sharper, no "here's what" filler |
| short-006 | "'You're so talented' is one of the most damaging things you can say…" | "Stop telling your kid they're talented. It backfires." | imperative + payoff up front; shorter to read in frame 1 |
| short-002 | "You finally won… so why do you feel empty?" | _(keep — strong curiosity gap as-is)_ | already front-loaded |
