## EMAIL 2 — The Night-Before Mistake
**Delay:** 1 day after Email 1
**Subject:** The mistake 90% of athletes make the night before
**Preview:** It's not nerves. It's something you're actively doing.

---

Hey [First Name],

Last night before a big competition, what do you do?

Most athletes do one of these:
- Watch replays of their best performances
- Over-analyze their opponent
- Try to visualize "winning"
- Run through every possible scenario

All of these are wrong. All of them spike cortisol and shred sleep quality.

**Here's what elite athletes do instead:**

They switch to deliberate boredom.

Light meal, low-stimulation activity (reading, casual walking), no screens after 9pm, and they're in bed at the same time they always are. Same as a Tuesday night.

Why? Because the brain treats novelty as a threat. Routine signals safety. Calm nervous system = better access to skill under pressure.

The protocol isn't about psyching yourself up. It's about staying exactly as calm as your training.

Tomorrow I'll share the specific breathing technique Novak Djokovic uses between points — and how you can adapt it to your sport.

— Giannis

**Preview:** 6 seconds. Works for any sport.

---

Hey [First Name],

Watch any Djokovic match closely.

Between points, after he wins one and especially after he loses one, he does the same thing: he walks to the back of the court, bounces on his toes, takes one slow breath, and bounces the ball exactly the same number of times.

It looks like a quirk. It's a precision tool.

**What it actually does:**
- The slow breath activates the parasympathetic nervous system (drops heart rate in ~6 seconds)
- The bouncing pattern reactivates motor cortex — brain shifts from emotional to mechanical
- The routine signals to the subconscious: "I've done this a thousand times. This is just another point."

You can build the same thing for your sport. A 6-second reset routine between plays, serves, rallies, or rounds. Practiced in training until it's automatic.

When pressure peaks, the routine runs. You don't have to think.
