# Cold Email

## Overview

A cold email earns a reply, not a sale. The only goal of the first email is a positive response. Short, specific to the recipient, one clear ask. Anything that reads like a mass blast gets deleted.

**Core principle:** Relevance beats persuasion. One sentence that proves you understand *their* situation outperforms three paragraphs about you.

## The Structure (keep it under ~90 words)

1. **Opener (1 line)** — a specific, true observation about *them* (their company, role, a recent event). Not "I hope you're well."
2. **Bridge (1–2 lines)** — connect that observation to a problem you solve. Lead with their outcome, not your features.
3. **Proof (optional, 1 line)** — a concrete result or relevant name. Specific numbers > adjectives.
4. **Ask (1 line)** — one low-friction CTA. "Worth a quick look?" / "Open to a 15-min call Thursday?" Never multiple asks.

## Subject Lines

- 2–4 words, lowercase ok, looks like a human wrote it to one person.
- No "Re:" fakery, no clickbait, no ALL CAPS, no emojis.
- Curiosity or relevance, not hype: `quick question about [their thing]`.

## Follow-ups

Most replies come from follow-ups, not the first email. 3–4 touches, spaced 3–5 days, each adding *new* value (a resource, a different angle) — never just "bumping this." Last touch is a polite breakup ("I'll close the loop on my end").

## Deliverability (or none of this matters)

- Plain text feel; ≤1 link; no image-heavy templates.
- Personalize a real variable, not just `{{first_name}}`.
- Verified list, warmed domain, sensible daily volume — spam-foldered = invisible.

## Common Mistakes

- Talking about yourself in the first line.
- Multiple CTAs / a calendar wall of times.
- Fake personalization ("loved your work!") with no specifics.
- Feature dumps instead of the recipient's outcome.
- Following up with "just checking in" and zero new value.
