# SMS Marketing

## Overview
SMS is intimate, high-open, and tightly regulated — earn opt-in, stay brief, and reserve it for messages that genuinely need immediacy. Abuse it and people unsubscribe instantly.

## Compliance (non-negotiable)
- **Explicit opt-in** required before any send; keep records.
- Include sender identity in early messages.
- Every message path must honor **STOP** to unsubscribe and HELP.
- Respect quiet hours; follow local regulations (TCPA, etc.).

## Writing Texts
1. **One message, one goal** — keep under ~160 chars when possible.
2. **Lead with the value/urgency** — "24h left: 20% off."
3. **Brand it** — say who it's from.
4. **One link** — short, trackable.
5. **Clear next step** — what to do, now.

## Timing
- Send during waking, non-quiet hours; respect time zones.
- Use for time-sensitive moments: flash sales, cart abandonment, drops, reminders, shipping.

## When SMS Beats Email
- Urgent/time-boxed offers and reminders.
- Transactional confirmations.
- When open speed matters (SMS opens in minutes vs. hours).

## Quick Reference
| Element | Rule |
|---------|------|
| Length | <160 chars ideal |
| Opt-in | Required, explicit |
| STOP/HELP | Always honored |
| Links | One, shortened |
| Timing | Waking hours, time zone |
| Frequency | Low — 2-4/month |

## Common Mistakes
- Texting without documented opt-in (illegal, fines).
- No STOP handling or hidden unsubscribe.
- Over-sending — fatigue and mass opt-outs.
- Long, multi-goal messages.
- Multiple links or unbranded "who is this?" texts.
- Sending during quiet hours / wrong time zone.
