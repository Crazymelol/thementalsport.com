# Content Strategy

## Overview

Content strategy connects business goals to what you publish, for whom, and why. Without it, you produce random posts that don't compound. With it, every piece serves a stage of the audience's journey and feeds the next.

**Core principle:** Start from the audience's problem and the business goal, not from "what should we post this week." Channels and formats are the last decision, not the first.

## The Planning Chain

1. **Goal** — what business outcome? (awareness, leads, sales, retention) Pick one primary per initiative.
2. **Audience** — who, and what do they already believe/struggle with? Be specific.
3. **Topics** — the questions they're asking, mapped to funnel stage:
   - **TOFU** (awareness): broad pains, "why" and "what" questions → discovery
   - **MOFU** (consideration): comparisons, how-tos, frameworks → trust
   - **BOFU** (decision): proof, demos, offers, objections → conversion
4. **Format & channel** — match topic + stage to where the audience already is (search, social, email, video).
5. **Cadence** — sustainable beats heroic. Consistency compounds; sporadic bursts don't.

## The Pillar + Repurpose Model

Create one substantial **pillar** asset (guide, video, research), then atomize it into many channel-native pieces (clips, threads, email, carousel). One idea → a week of content. Don't create every piece from scratch.

|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Plan in themes/months, not isolated posts. Leave room for timely/reactive content.

## Measuring

Tie each piece to its stage's metric — don't judge a TOFU awareness post by direct sales. Track: reach (TOFU), engagement/email signups (MOFU), conversions (BOFU). Kill what doesn't move its metric; double down on what does.

## Common Mistakes

- Publishing by format ("we need a blog") instead of by audience question.
- All TOFU, no MOFU/BOFU → traffic that never converts.
- New idea every time instead of repurposing a pillar.
- Unsustainable cadence that collapses in a month.
- No CTA / no next step from any given piece.
