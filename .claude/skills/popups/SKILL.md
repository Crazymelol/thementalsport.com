# On-Site Popups & Opt-Ins

## Overview
A popup should interrupt at the right moment with a relevant offer and minimal friction — value to the visitor, not just a demand for their email. Bad timing and greed kill both conversion and trust.

## Trigger Strategy
- **Exit-intent** (desktop) — catch leavers without interrupting active readers.
- **Scroll depth** — fire at 50-70% (visitor showed interest).
- **Time delay** — only after meaningful engagement (15-30s+), never instant.
- **Click/inline** — least intrusive; user-initiated.
- Frequency-cap: show once, suppress after dismiss/convert, respect mobile rules.

## Offer & Form
1. **Specific offer** — discount, lead magnet, or clear benefit; "10% off" beats "subscribe."
2. **One field** — email only; every added field cuts conversion.
3. **Strong CTA** — value-based button ("Get my 10%"), not "Submit."
4. **Easy dismiss** — visible close; honest "no thanks" copy.

## Quick Reference
| Trigger | Best for | When |
|---------|----------|------|
| Exit-intent | Cart/leavers | Desktop only |
| Scroll 50-70% | Engaged readers | Blog/content |
| Time delay | General | After 15-30s |
| Inline/click | Low friction | Anytime |

## Common Mistakes
- Firing instantly on page load before any value is delivered.
- Asking for too many fields.
- Aggressive exit-intent on mobile (Google penalizes intrusive interstitials).
- No frequency cap — same popup every visit.
- Hidden or dark-pattern close buttons.
- Vague offer ("join our newsletter") with no reason to.
