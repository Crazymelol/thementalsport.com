# Ad Creative

## Overview
The creative is the targeting. A scroll-stopping hook in the first 1-3 seconds (or top third of a static) drives most performance — ship many concepts, not many tweaks of one.

## Framework
1. **Hook (first 3s / headline)** — interrupt the scroll. Lead with the problem, a bold claim, motion, faces, or a pattern break. Never open with a logo or slow brand intro.
2. **Retention** — deliver value fast: demo, transformation, or proof. Cut every dead frame.
3. **Body** — one core message tied to one customer pain. Show, don't list.
4. **CTA** — explicit ask plus reason to act now.
5. **Format fit** — vertical 9:16 for TikTok/Reels/Stories; square or 4:5 for feed. Native-looking beats produced on social.

## Quick Reference
| Goal | Best format | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| Cold awareness | UGC / founder talking head | Trust + native feel |
| Proof / conversion | Testimonial, before/after | Social proof |
| Retargeting | Offer-led static | Speed, clear discount |
| TikTok | Raw UGC, trends, captions | Avoid polished |
| Meta feed | 4:5, text overlay, hook | Caption first line = hook |

**Hook archetypes:** problem call-out, "I tried X so you don't have to", curiosity gap, bold/contrarian claim, before/after, listicle ("3 reasons...").

## Common Mistakes
- One ad with 20 tweaks instead of 5 distinct concepts/angles.
- Slow branded intro before the hook.
- Over-polished video where UGC outperforms.
- No captions/subtitles (most watch muted).
- Cramming multiple messages — one ad, one idea.
- Wrong aspect ratio cropping the hook.
- Judging creative on vanity metrics instead of CTR, thumb-stop rate, hook rate, and CAC.
