# Marketing & Lifecycle Email

## Overview
You're writing to people who opted in, so earn the open and the next open — deliver value, keep one clear goal per email, and protect deliverability by sending wanted mail. (Cold outreach to non-subscribers is a different discipline.)

## Email Types
- **Welcome** — deliver the promised value immediately, set expectations, build the habit of opening.
- **Nurture** — sequence that educates/builds trust toward an offer; value before ask.
- **Promo** — time-bound offer; clear deadline and single CTA.
- **Broadcast/newsletter** — recurring value; one main idea, lightly personal.

## Structure
1. **Subject line** — curiosity, benefit, or specificity; 30-50 chars; no clickbait.
2. **Preview text** — extends the subject, never wasted.
3. **Open** — one hook, get to the point.
4. **Body** — one idea, skimmable, conversational.
5. **Single CTA** — one clear action.

## Deliverability Basics
- Authenticate (SPF, DKIM, DMARC).
- Clean list; remove non-openers; honor unsubscribes instantly.
- Avoid spam triggers, all-caps, excessive links/images.
- Warm up new sending domains; watch engagement signals.

## Quick Reference
| Type | Goal | Frequency | CTA |
|------|------|-----------|-----|
| Welcome | Activate | Immediate | Consume value |
| Nurture | Build trust | Every 2-4 days | Soft → hard |
| Promo | Convert | During offer | Buy now |
| Newsletter | Retain | Weekly/biweekly | Read/reply |

## Common Mistakes
- Multiple competing CTAs in one email.
- Subject lines that overpromise and tank trust.
- Treating subscribers like cold leads (over-pitching too early).
- Ignoring preview text.
- Sending to unengaged contacts and harming sender reputation.
- Walls of text with no skimmable structure.
