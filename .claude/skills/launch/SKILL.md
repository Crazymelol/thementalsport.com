# Launch Sequences

## Overview
A launch manufactures attention and urgency around a window — anticipation before, momentum during, and a real deadline at close, coordinated across every channel.

## Phases
1. **Pre-launch (anticipation)** — tease, build a waitlist, share the why/story, seed problem-awareness. Collect interested leads before selling.
2. **Open / launch** — announce, open cart, lead with transformation and proof. Hit hardest on day 1 and final day.
3. **Mid-launch** — handle objections, social proof, FAQ, case studies, bonuses.
4. **Cart-close (urgency)** — deadline-driven; "last chance," "24h," "few hours left," final send. Most sales land here.

## Multi-Channel Coordination
- Email carries the heavy lifting; sequence each phase.
- Social builds public momentum and FOMO; SMS for urgent close reminders.
- Same message, phase-appropriate, native per channel.
- Stack send frequency toward open and close.

## Quick Reference
| Phase | Goal | Channels | Frequency |
|-------|------|----------|-----------|
| Pre-launch | Waitlist, desire | Social, email | Building |
| Open | Announce, sell | Email, all | High day 1 |
| Mid | Objections, proof | Email, social | Steady |
| Close | Urgency | Email, SMS | Heaviest |

## Common Mistakes
- No pre-launch — opening cart to a cold audience.
- Fake or soft deadlines that destroy future urgency.
- Going quiet at cart-close (where most sales happen) for fear of "annoying."
- Same message every send instead of escalating angles.
- Channels acting in isolation, not coordinated.
- Selling features instead of transformation and proof.
