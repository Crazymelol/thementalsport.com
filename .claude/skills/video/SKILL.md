# Short-Form Video Content

## Overview
Short-form lives or dies on the first 1-2 seconds. The hook earns the next second; every second must earn the next. Optimize for retention curve, not production polish.

## Structure
1. **Hook (0-2s)**: Open mid-action or with a bold claim/question. Show the payoff or stakes immediately. No logos, no slow intros.
2. **Setup (2-5s)**: Frame the tension or promise. Tell them what they'll get.
3. **Payload (5-40s)**: Deliver value in tight beats. One idea per beat. Cut hard between beats.
4. **Loop/CTA (last 1-3s)**: End so it loops cleanly or drives a clear next action (follow, comment, save).

## Retention tactics
- Pattern interrupts every 2-4s: zoom, cut, B-roll, text pop, sound effect.
- Open loops: tease a payoff you resolve later in the clip.
- Captions always-on (most watch muted). Burn them in, sync to speech.
- B-roll covers every spoken claim; never let a talking head sit static.
- Keep pace fast: trim breaths, ums, and dead air ruthlessly.

## Quick Reference
| Element | Target |
|---|---|
| Hook length | 1-2s |
| Aspect ratio | 9:16 (1080x1920) |
| Total length | 15-45s (test 7-90s) |
| Caption font | Large, high-contrast, lower-third or center |
| Cut cadence | Every 2-4s |
| First frame | Visually loaded, no blank intro |

## Common Mistakes
- Slow intros, brand bumpers, or "hey guys" before the hook.
- No captions, or tiny captions.
- Static shot held too long with no B-roll or motion.
- Burying the payoff at the end with no tease up front.
- One idea stretched too thin; pad kills retention.
- Ignoring the loop/CTA ending.
