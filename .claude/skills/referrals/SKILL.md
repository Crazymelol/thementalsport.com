# Referrals & Viral Loops

## Overview
Referrals work when sharing is easy, the reward is meaningful to both sides, and the moment to ask aligns with peak satisfaction. Aim to shorten the loop and lift the viral coefficient (K).

## Framework
1. **Make the product worth sharing** — referrals amplify love; they can't manufacture it.
2. **Design the incentive** — double-sided rewards (giver + receiver) outperform one-sided. Match reward type to product (credit, cash, upgrades, status).
3. **Pick the moment** — prompt at peak delight (post-purchase, aha, positive review/NPS).
4. **Reduce friction** — one-tap share, prefilled message, personal link, multi-channel (SMS, WhatsApp, email, social).
5. **Optimize the loop** — track invites sent → accepted → activated; raise K = invites × conversion. Shorten cycle time.
6. **Prevent abuse** — fraud checks, reward on referred-user activation not signup.

## Quick Reference
| Element | Best practice |
|---------|---------------|
| Reward | Double-sided, meaningful, instant-ish |
| Trigger | Peak satisfaction moment |
| Mechanic | One-tap, prefilled, personal link |
| Metric | Viral coefficient K, cycle time |
| Payout | On referred-user activation, not signup |

## Common Mistakes
- Launching referrals on a product people don't love yet.
- One-sided rewards (only the referrer benefits).
- Rewards too small to motivate or too generous to be abused.
- High-friction sharing (manual codes, no prefilled message).
- Asking at the wrong moment (before value).
- No fraud prevention → self-referrals drain budget.
- Not measuring K or activation, so you can't tell if it works.
