# MODULE 1 — FOUNDATION: THE INNER GAME
*4 lessons · 32 min · Featured: Kobe Bryant, Phil Jackson, Bill Russell*

## Lesson 1.1 — Why 90% of Performance Is Mental

**Hook (camera):** "In 1998, Michael Jordan made the game-winning shot of the NBA Finals with a 103-degree fever and the flu. Same body, same shot, same court — Jordan's mind made him a god that night. Talent didn't win that game. Mind did."

**The Lesson:** Every elite performer admits the same thing — at the top, physical ability between competitors is nearly identical. The difference is mental. Bill Russell won 11 NBA championships. He vomited before nearly every game. The fear didn't disappear — he just didn't let it choose for him.

**Three things you'll master in this course:**
1. Pre-event preparation (Module 2 — the 7-day Competition Protocol)
2. In-event execution (Modules 4, 5, 7)
3. Post-event recovery & growth (Modules 6, 8)

**Action:** Write down your last performance failure. Was it physical, technical, or mental? Be honest.

---

## Lesson 1.2 — The Three Enemies: Fear, Doubt, Distraction

**Story — Kobe & The Mamba Mentality:** Kobe Bryant didn't have natural confidence. He manufactured it. He named his fear-fighting alter ego "Black Mamba" — a venomous snake that strikes with precision. When fear came, Kobe didn't suppress it. He named it. He gave it a face. Then he beat it.

**The 3 Enemies framework:**
- **FEAR** — physical sensation (heart rate, tunnel vision). Lives in the body.
- **DOUBT** — narrative voice ("I'm not ready"). Lives in the mind.
- **DISTRACTION** — external pull (crowd, ref, opponent). Lives in the environment.

Each has a different antidote. Most athletes fight all three with willpower. Willpower fails. Protocols don't.

**Action:** Identify which of the 3 hits you hardest. That's your starting battle.

---

## Lesson 1.3 — Build Your Mental Performance Profile

**The Profile (your worksheet):** Answer in writing:
1. What's the highest-pressure moment you've ever performed in?
2. What did your body feel like? (heart, breath, hands, vision)
3. What did your mind say to you?
4. What did you do that worked?
5. What did you do that hurt you?

**This becomes your baseline.** Phil Jackson made every Bulls and Lakers player keep a journal. The Mental Performance Profile is your version.

---

## Lesson 1.4 — The Protocol Mindset (Why Routines Beat Motivation)

**Story:** Eliud Kipchoge breaks marathon records by doing the SAME warm-up, eating the SAME food, going to bed at the SAME time. He doesn't run on motivation. Motivation is weather. Protocol is climate.

**Core principle:** Discipline → Protocol → Automation → Excellence

When excellence is automated, fear has nothing to grip.

# MODULE 2 — THE COMPETITION PROTOCOL (T-MINUS 7 DAYS)
*7 lessons · 42 min · Featured: GSP, Tom Brady, Eliud Kipchoge*

## Lesson 2.1 — Day 7: The Mamba Contract

**Story:** Kobe didn't show up game day and try. He signed a contract with his future self a week before. He committed in writing — "I will outwork the man across from me. I will not flinch."

**The Protocol:**
1. Write your specific outcome ("I will run the 5k under 18:30")
2. Write your specific behaviors ("I will visualize the start 10 min daily")
3. Sign and date it
4. Tape it where you see it every morning

## Lesson 2.2 — Day 6: Audio Conditioning

**Story:** Tom Brady listened to the same playlist before every Patriots game for 20 years. Same songs, same order. By the time the music started, his nervous system already knew what was coming.

**The Protocol:**
1. Pick 5 songs that put you in a calm-but-ready state (NOT pump-up rage)
2. Always play them in the same order, same volume, same headphones
3. Within 3 weeks, song 1 = ready state.

## Lesson 2.3 — Day 5: Breath Control (The Physiological Sigh)

Discovered by Stanford neuroscientist Andrew Huberman. Two short inhales through nose, one long exhale through mouth. Drops heart rate within 60 seconds.

**Protocol:** 5 cycles before sleep, 5 upon waking, 3 in the locker room, 1 at the line.

## Lesson 2.4 — Day 4: Visualizing Disaster (Negative Visualization)

**Story:** Michael Phelps' coach Bob Bowman made him visualize disasters his entire life. Goggles fill with water? Visualized. Cramp? Visualized. At Beijing 2008, his goggles DID fill with water during the 200m butterfly. He swam blind, by feel, by the visualization. He won gold and broke the world record.

**Protocol:**
1. Pick the 3 worst things that could happen in your event
2. Visualize each disaster vividly (5 min)
3. Then visualize your recovery (5 min)
4. Repeat for 7 nights before the event

When the disaster comes (and it always comes), you've already lived through it.

## Lesson 2.5 — Day 3: The Anchor (Trigger Flow Instantly)

Behavioral conditioning. Pair a specific physical movement with a peak state, repeat 100 times, the movement triggers the state.

**Protocol:**
1. Pick a unique gesture (touch left wrist, tap chest twice)
2. Each peak training moment, perform the gesture
3. Within 4-6 weeks, the gesture alone triggers the state
4. Use it pre-shot, pre-set, pre-race

This is what Tiger Woods does on the tee. Watch any pro tennis player serve.

## Lesson 2.6 — Day 2: Gear Audit & SOPs

- Lay out every piece of gear the night before
- Photograph it (so packing for travel becomes copy-paste)
- Standard Operating Procedure for warm-up: same time, order, duration
- Eliminate decision fatigue

## Lesson 2.7 — Day 1: Final Hours — Thinking Is The Enemy

**Story:** GSP's mantra: "When the cage closes, thinking is the enemy." Mike Tyson: "Everyone has a plan until they get punched in the face."

**The Last 60 Minutes:**
- 60 min out: silence, no phone
- 30 min: warm-up SOP
- 10 min: anchor + breath protocol
- 5 min: visualize first 30 seconds of execution
- 0: trust the protocol, let go

# MODULE 3 — RESILIENT CONFIDENCE
*4 lessons · 40 min · Featured: Serena Williams, Lionel Messi, Roger Federer*

## Lesson 3.1 — Confidence Is a Skill, Not a Feeling

**Story:** Serena Williams lost 4 Grand Slam finals in a row early in her career. Most players spiral. Serena rebuilt confidence through DAILY confidence reps — winning small, measurable moments. You don't wait for confidence. You earn it in 5-minute increments.

## Lesson 3.2 — The "Give Your Best" Philosophy

**Story:** Roger Federer's approach: "I don't try to win. I try to play my best tennis. If my best beats his best, I win. If not, I have nothing to be ashamed of."

This frees you from the result. Outcome confidence is fragile. Process confidence is unbreakable.

## Lesson 3.3 — Daily Confidence Reps (The 5-Win Protocol)

Every morning, write 5 specific things you'll do today that you can definitely complete. They must be small enough to be guaranteed wins.
- Drink 1 glass of water immediately
- Make my bed
- Send 1 grateful text
- 10 push-ups
- Read 1 page

End of day: Check them off. 5 wins per day = 1,825 wins per year. Confidence compounds.

## Lesson 3.4 — Failure Without Identity Collapse

**Story:** Messi missed a penalty in the 2016 Copa America final, retired from international football for a year. Then he came back, won the 2022 World Cup. Failure is data, not identity.

**Protocol:** After a loss, write 3 sentences:
1. What happened (facts only)
2. What I'll change (1 specific thing)
3. What's still true about me (your unchanged identity)

# MODULE 4 — VISUALIZING DISASTER (AND RECOVERING)
*3 lessons · 36 min · Featured: Michael Phelps, Lindsey Vonn, Wayne Gretzky*

## Lesson 4.1 — Negative Visualization (The Stoic Edge)

Phelps' Beijing 2008 — see Module 2.4. Bowman would do "horrible" practices — turning off pool lights, breaking goggles, sabotaging alarms. By Olympic time, nothing could surprise Phelps.

## Lesson 4.2 — Recovery Scripts for Failure

Pre-write 3 scripts for the 3 most likely failures in your sport.
Example for tennis:
- After double fault → "Reset. Same toss next serve. One point doesn't define the match."
- After losing a set → "Marathon, not sprint. Adjust serve speed by 10%."
- Bad call → "Next point. The umpire isn't winning the match. I am."

Read them daily for 7 days before competition.

## Lesson 4.3 — Bouncing Back in Real Time (90-Second Rule)

Neuroscientist Jill Bolte Taylor: emotions physically clear the body in 90 seconds — IF you don't keep feeding them with thought.

**Protocol when something goes wrong:**
- Acknowledge ("That hurt")
- Breathe physiological sigh × 3
- Anchor (gesture from Module 2.5)
- Next play

90 seconds. Then you're back.

# MODULE 5 — BREATH, RITUALS, AND FLOW TRIGGERS
*5 lessons · 40 min · Featured: Wim Hof, Djokovic, Steph Curry*

## Lesson 5.1 — Box Breathing (Navy SEAL Standard)
4 in, 4 hold, 4 out, 4 hold. Repeat 4 cycles. Used by US Navy SEALs to drop heart rate before missions.

## Lesson 5.2 — The Physiological Sigh (Mastery)
Same as Module 2.3, practiced until automatic. 200 reps over 30 days = autonomic.

## Lesson 5.3 — Ritual Stack (The Federer 11)

Roger Federer touches his hair, adjusts shirt, bounces ball exactly 4 times — IDENTICAL every serve for 20 years. Not superstition. Conditioning.

**Protocol:** Build YOUR 5-step ritual. Do it 100 times in practice. It becomes the bridge between thinking and execution.

## Lesson 5.4 — Flow State Triggers

Per Steven Kotler's research, flow has 17 triggers. Top 3 for athletes:
1. Clear goals (know exactly what success looks like next 60 sec)
2. Immediate feedback (your senses, not your coach)
3. Challenge slightly above skill (4% rule)

## Lesson 5.5 — The Wim Hof Protocol (Cold + Breath)

Wim Hof climbed Kilimanjaro in shorts.

**Beginner Protocol:**
- 30 deep breaths (in deep, out gentle)
- Exhale fully and hold (1-2 min)
- Recover with deep inhale and hold 15 sec
- 3 rounds, daily
- Cold shower (30 sec) at end

Athletic transfer: 3-5 weeks.

# MODULE 6 — THE PHYSIOLOGY LAYER
*4 lessons · 40 min · Featured: LeBron James, Cristiano Ronaldo, Tom Brady*

## Lesson 6.1 — Sleep Architecture (LeBron's $1.5M Habit)

LeBron sleeps 12 hours during season. Reportedly $1.5M/year on body — sleep is the cheapest one.

**Protocol:**
- Same bedtime within 30-min window, 7 days/week
- Bedroom 65-68°F (18-20°C)
- No screens 60 min before bed
- Magnesium + glycine 30 min before bed
- Aim: 8.5 hours minimum on training days

## Lesson 6.2 — Nutrition Timing
- Pre-event (3h): carbs + small protein
- Pre-event (60 min): banana + small dates
- During (>90 min event): 30-60g carbs/hour
- Post (30 min window): 20-30g protein + carbs

Brady eats this way. Ronaldo too.

## Lesson 6.3 — Recovery Protocols
- Cold plunge: 50-59°F × 3 min
- Sauna: 170-200°F × 15-20 min × 4×/week
- Walking: 10k steps day after event flushes lactate

## Lesson 6.4 — Caffeine, Hydration, Supplements
- **Caffeine:** 3-6mg/kg, 30-60 min pre. NOT daily. Build tolerance breaks.
- **Hydration:** Body weight × 0.6 = oz/day baseline. Add electrolytes for >60 min sweat.
- **Top 4 supplements:** Creatine 5g, Vitamin D3 2000 IU, Omega-3 2g, Magnesium glycinate 400mg.

# MODULE 7 — MENTAL TOUGHNESS IN REAL TIME
*5 lessons · 40 min · Featured: Muhammad Ali, Simone Biles, Usain Bolt*

## Lesson 7.1 — Hacking the Crowd (Ali's Trick)

Ali fought in Zaire ("Rumble in the Jungle") with the entire crowd chanting "Ali bomaye!" (Ali, kill him). He turned the noise into rocket fuel.

**Protocol:** Pre-decide before any event — crowd noise = my fuel, regardless of what they're saying.

## Lesson 7.2 — Bad Refs / Unfair Calls

Pre-commit: "I will not give a referee my emotional energy."
When a bad call happens:
- 90-second rule (Module 4.3)
- Anchor
- Next play

## Lesson 7.3 — Trash Talk Firewall

Conor McGregor talked endless trash to Khabib. Khabib never engaged. Khabib won.

3 lines you can use:
1. "Thanks for the energy."
2. (Smile, nothing else)
3. "Save it for after the match."

Then immediately back to your protocol.

## Lesson 7.4 — The Twisties (When Your Body Refuses)

Simone Biles withdrew from Tokyo 2020 with "the twisties" — her body literally lost spatial awareness mid-air. She did the right thing: stop. Forcing through psychological injury creates physical injury.

**Listen for the Twisties:**
- Sudden inability to do a move you've done 10,000 times
- Spatial disorientation
- Sense of "wrongness" in body
→ Stop. Reset. Don't force. Get help.

## Lesson 7.5 — The Taper (Final 72 Hours)

- 72h out: cut training volume by 50%
- 48h out: light skill work only
- 24h out: total rest, only mental rehearsal
- Day of: warm-up SOP, nothing more

Most athletes overtrain in the final week and arrive depleted.

# MODULE 8 — MASTERY & MENTORSHIP
*4 lessons · 40 min · Featured: Gregg Popovich, John Wooden, Phil Jackson*

## Lesson 8.1 — The Monthly Reset

1. List wins this month (specific)
2. List losses (specific)
3. Identify ONE pattern you'll break
4. Identify ONE habit you'll add
5. Update Mental Performance Profile (Module 1.3)

Same review process Phil Jackson did with players after every season.

## Lesson 8.2 — Building Your Own Protocols

This course gives you templates. Mastery is making them YOURS.

**Process:**
- Take any protocol
- Run it for 30 days as written
- Then change ONE variable
- Run it 30 more days
- Compare. Keep what worked.

## Lesson 8.3 — Teaching to Master (Mentorship)

Gregg Popovich (5× NBA Champion coach) is teaching the next generation of coaches at every practice. The deepest learning happens through teaching.

- Find one younger athlete to mentor (informally)
- Teach them ONE protocol from this course
- Watch how they learn it
- You'll see your own gaps

## Lesson 8.4 — The Lifetime Athlete Mindset

The point was never to be perfect for one event. The point is to keep showing up — for the next session, the next season, the next decade. The athletes featured all had one thing in common: they kept doing the work after the cameras turned off.

You now have the same toolkit they have. Use it.

— Giannis Notaras
